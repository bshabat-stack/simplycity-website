Our GBP optimization process — published in full

Most agencies treat their process as a trade secret. Ours follows the public research consensus (Google's own guidance, Whitespark's local ranking factor studies) plus our own data from 200+ managed locations — so there's nothing to hide. Here is the actual order of operations:

1
Step 1
Keyword core and map intent
Before touching the profile, we identify the 10 high-intent searches that should bring you customers, and map each one to its job: category choice, services list, landing page, or review prompts. We also review your top 3 Map Pack competitors. Strategy first — everything else inherits from it.
2
Step 2
Entity hygiene
Duplicate listings, wrong pins, inconsistent name/address/phone across the web — these quietly cap your ranking no matter how good the profile looks. We verify your business data everywhere it appears before building anything new.
3
Step 3
The high-impact profile fields
Not all GBP fields matter equally. Industry studies rank primary category, reviews, the linked website page, hours, services, and photos as the strongest levers — while descriptions, social links, and Google Posts sit far lower. We weight our work accordingly: the heavy-impact fields get done first and best.
4
Step 4
Reviews as an operating system
A steady flow of recent reviews outperforms occasional bursts. We build review requests into your customer journey — including NFC tap-to-review tools placed at your counter — monitor recency, respond to every review, and run a defined workflow for negative ones.
5
Step 5
The landing page connection
Your profile links to a page on your website, and that page's relevance affects your Maps ranking. We make sure the linked page matches the searches you're targeting — title tag, headings, and content — or flag exactly what's missing.
6
Step 6
Listings, citations, and local mentions
We clean up existing directory listings first (old addresses, duplicates), then build new ones — general, niche, and local directories — plus local mentions and backlinks where opportunities exist.
7
Step 7
Monthly operating cadence
Profiles decay: Google applies suggested edits, photos go stale, hours change, competitors move. Every month we monitor edits, publish posts and photos, track rankings and performance, and send you a report in plain language.
